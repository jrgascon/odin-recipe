Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian baked pasta dish made with alternating layers of wide, flat pasta sheets, rich meat or vegetable ragù, creamy béchamel sauce, and melted cheese (typically mozzarella and Parmesan). The layers are baked until golden and bubbly, creating a comforting, hearty casserole with a perfect balance of textures—tender pasta, savory filling, and crispy cheese topping. It's a beloved comfort food worldwide, often served at family gatherings and special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef (or mix beef/pork)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 carrot, finely chopped
  • 1 celery stalk, finely chopped
  • 28 oz (800g) canned crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 cup red wine (optional)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Fresh basil, oregano, salt, and black pepper
  • 1 bay leaf

Preparations

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat
  2. Sauté onion, carrot, and celery until softened (5-7 minutes)
  3. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant
  4. Add ground meat and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon
  5. Stir in tomato paste and cook for 2 minutes
  6. Pour in red wine (if using) and simmer until reduced by half
  7. Add crushed tomatoes, herbs, bay leaf, salt, and pepper
  8. Simmer uncovered for 1-2 hours, stirring occasionally, until thick
